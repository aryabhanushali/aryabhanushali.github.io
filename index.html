Face-Selective Neuron Experiment

This project explores how face-selective neurons work in a brain-inspired deep neural network (TopoNets). Using the experiment notebook (faceSelectiveNeuronsExperiment.ipynb), I identify which neurons respond most strongly to face images, measure how selective they are compared to other image types, and test if they are necessary for accurate face recognition. To simulate prosopagnosia (face blindness), I performed lesion experiments where face-selective neurons were ‚Äúzeroed out‚Äù to see how much the network‚Äôs accuracy on face tasks drops. I also compared neuron activations on face, scene, and object datasets, ran PCA to study how much variance the neurons explain, and used Torch-Dreams to visualize what kind of image patterns maximally activate them.

Technical stack: Python, PyTorch, TopoNets, Hugging Face Datasets, torchvision, einops, scikit-learn (PCA), matplotlib, seaborn, Torch-Dreams.
