Medtronic — Hugo Surgical Robotics

Software Engineering Intern · New Haven, CT

Joining the team behind the Hugo robotic-assisted surgery system. This is where AI for healthcare gets to leave the screen and actually move with a surgeon's hand.

Murty Lab — Undergraduate Researcher

Feb 2025 — Present · Georgia Tech

CNNs are famously black-box models — you can run them, but you can't really see what they've learned. I wanted to change that. So I built what I call a "neural microscope": using feature visualization to generate the exact images each neuron "wants to see" most. Suddenly the network stops being a black box — you can stare at what a face-selective neuron has decided faces look like.

I also helped build Cortex-Canvas, an interactive web tool that lets neuroscientists and students poke at brain-region models to test their own hypotheses about visual cortex. My contribution: an AI agent that sits inside the tool and guides experiments — suggesting what to try next, navigating the parameter space, making the whole thing feel less like reading a manual and more like talking to a colleague. (CHI EA '26 paper →)

Bio-MIBLab — Undergraduate Researcher

2025 — Present · Georgia Tech

Asking whether temporal knowledge graphs — graphs that change with time — can pick up the early signals of disease that snapshot models miss. A patient's medical history isn't a list of events, it's a trajectory, and the shape of that trajectory carries information that standard tabular models throw away.

Elevance Health — AI & Digital Platforms Intern

May 2025 — Aug 2025 · Atlanta, GA

The whole summer was about turning workflows into agents. Two systems shipped:

(1) An invoice-validation pipeline that reads incoming invoices, checks them against contracts and historical patterns, and flags discrepancies — found $15K of mismatched line items in our first pilot.

(2) An SDLC agent chain: a Jira agent that generates tickets, hands them off to a Dev / QA / Deployment chain where the next agent picks up the work. We were trying to answer: can an AI team handle its own sprint planning?

Bhasin Biomedicine Lab — AI/ML Intern

Jan 2022 — Jan 2024 · Atlanta, GA

My first time using code to look at actual cells. Histopathology slides live on physical glass — to see them digitally, you scan them at high magnification, but each scan only captures a tiny tile. I wrote the Python/OpenCV pipeline that stitched thousands of those tiles into a single navigable whole-slide image — digital pathology, built from scratch.

Then we trained a TensorFlow CNN to detect lymphocyte infiltrates — clusters of T cells the immune system sends in around malignant tissue. The model learned to spot the body mid-fight, which is one of the earliest signals pathologists look for. That project is why I care about healthcare AI in the first place.
