Agentic AI, LangChain, and AI Workflows

June 7, 2025 • Arya Bhanushali • Agentic AI, LangChain, LLM Frameworks

Lately, I’ve been exploring the difference between basic AI workflows and what people are now calling Agentic AI. Understanding that distinction, and tools like LangChain that help bring it to life, has opened up a whole new way of thinking about how AI systems can behave and their capabilities.

AI Workflow vs. Agentic AI

In a traditional AI workflow, you send a prompt to a model and get a response. There is a human decision-maker. Maybe you validate or transform that response using some rules or structure. It’s linear, predictable, and controlled, useful for well-defined tasks like extracting data or answering a direct question.

Agentic AI, on the other hand, goes a step further. It involves systems that can decide what to do based on a goal. So now an LLM is the decision-maker that reasons. These systems use language models not just to generate responses, but to plan steps, choose tools, act, and adapt to feedback.

Enter LangChain

LangChain is a powerful framework that makes it easier to build these kinds of agentic systems. It connects large language models (like GPT-4) to tools, APIs, memory, and document retrievers. With LangChain, you’re not just prompting a model, you’re building a thinking system.

I’ve been learning how LangChain allows you to:

  • Build retrieval-augmented pipelines that ground LLMs in external knowledge
  • Create custom tools that the model can use dynamically
  • Add memory to retain context across steps or sessions
  • Construct agents that reason through complex, multi-step goals

Why This Matters

The shift from prompt engineering to agent design feels like a natural evolution in AI development. It’s not just about getting better responses, but now getting better steps in the first place.
